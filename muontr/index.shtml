Transition Radiation from Cosmic Muons

[Back to homepage]

This figure shows the signal of the Transition Radiation Detector (TRD) in the ALICE experiment at the LHC. The data are from various sources:

  • (blue points, magenta squares) pions and electrons from the testbeam measurements with prototype chambers [1],
  • (green stars) protons, pions and electrons from proton-proton collisions at the LHC [2],
  • and (red and black triangles) muons from cosmic ray measurements [3].
In [3] you can find the answers to the following questions concerning the muon measurements which were performed during the pauses between the LHC collisions:
  • How did we manage to measure the momentum up to 1 TeV/c only using the ALICE TPC?
  • How did we "switch on" and "off" the transition radiation (TR) without even "touching" the detector? (dE/dx is a jargon for the collisional ionization.)
