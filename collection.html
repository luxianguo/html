• It is difficult to determine nuclear effects in neutrino-nucleus interactions because of the unknown neutrino energy—not if we look right into the flight direction of the neutrino.
    • Measurement of nuclear effects in neutrino interactions with minimal dependence on neutrino energy, Phys. Rev. C 94, 015503 (2016), arXiv:1512.05748 [nucl-th].
    • Theoretical predictions of transverse kinematic imbalance in neutrino-nucleus interactions (poster, proceedings@NuPhys2015).
    • Probing nuclear effects using single-transverse kinematic imbalance with MINERvA (poster, proceedings@Neutrino 2016).

  • To understand oscillation properties of a neutrino, it is crucial to measure its energy precisely. It is not impossible to circumvent nuclear effects—an obstacle that is in its own right an active field of research.
    • Reconstruction of energy spectra of neutrino beams independent of nuclear effects, Phys. Rev. D 92, 051302 (2015), arXiv:1507.00967 [hep-ex].
    • Reconstruction of Energy Spectra of Neutrino Beams Independent of Nuclear Effects: Prospects for Current Experiments (talk, proceedings@NuInt15).

  • Concept and development of a novel particle identification method with the Time Projection Chamber (TPC)- the TPC Coherent Fit. This method simultaneously performs dE/dx calibration and particle yield extraction for particles with momentum of 0.15-30 GeV/c.
    • The first measurement of charged pion, kaon and (anti-)proton production in jets from hadron colliders (talk, proceedings@Quark Matter 2014).
